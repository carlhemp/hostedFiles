Growing up, my family (Carl) would make a handmade Christmas card, each individually crayon-colored, that we would send out with our Christmas picture and a family newsletter. It's something that I always enjoyed as a Christmas tradition, and hoped to someday do with my family.

This spring I read through "The 3D Gospel: Ministry in Guilt, Shame, and Fear Cultures" by Jayson Georges. In his outline, western culture tends to be guilt-based, eastern culture tends to be shame-based, and tribal cultures tend to be fear-based cultures. Each culture and individual of the world operates out of a combination of these (and probably other) worldviews.

As I was reading and thinking about how to put together our card I saw a theme of God's redemption of mankind portrayed through His covering our brokenness and sin throughout scripture.

  • God covered Adam and Eve's shame when they sinned in the garden.
  • the people of Israel experienced God's power overcoming (covering) fear in their weakness before Pharaoh.
  • in Jesus' coming to earth, and especially His death on the cross, we clearly see God’s covering of our guilt.

God's work in redemption, and Jesus' life and death, accomplished far more than our salvation from guilt. It restores us into a right relationship in the family of God! Freed from all shame we acquire honor as his children, and access to the throne of God.

From all of us to you, Merry Christmas and may God bless you! Carl, Bridget, and Henry

P.S. We both colored, I worked on layout, Bridget did the artwork (no way I can make something this nice!), and Henry mostly just slept :-D.
